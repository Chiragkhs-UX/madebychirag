I am?
Then
Now

I was the kid in the last bench who found better things to do than study. My dad ran out of patience and sent me to a hostel — probably his best decision on my behalf.

Living alone at that age does something to you. I started observing people — quietly, carefully. How they talked, what they wanted, why they did things. My board marks surprised everyone, including me.

ICFAI Hyderabad, then an MBA, then growth work, then a second masters at Deakin in Melbourne. Each move felt like following a thread I couldn't quite name.

These days I spend a lot of time thinking about why people do what they do — and how to make that experience a little less frustrating for them. That's what design became for me. Not a craft I chose, more like a language I found that fit how I already thought.
I get a lot of energy from teaching and sharing. Not the formal kind — more like pulling up a chair and saying "here's what I figured out, you can have it." Knowledge feels better in motion.
Still curious. Still watching people. Just doing it with better tools now.
